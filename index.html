About

The STEM Junction Symposium is an annual bioinformatics symposium for advanced high school students in the Pittsburgh area. Bioinformatics is a new field that uses computer science and math to revolutionize the way biologists do research. The symposium is packed with talks on the newest discoveries and applications in sub-fields such as machine learning, computational modeling, gene discovery, electronic health records, data science ethics, and many more! The STEM Junction Symposium is designed by high school students for high school students, and it is the field trip that will influence career decisions and change lives. No prior experience in computer science or biology is necessary!

Mentors

We're looking for undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral students to mentor! See below for more information and how to sign up.

We recommend that our mentors...

1.) Are knowledgeble in bioinformatics and/or computational biology.

An understanding in bioinformatics or computational biology allows mentors to answer questions for students about lecture topics and the field in general.

2.) Can spend the whole day.

Mentors must be available for the entirety of the symposium. They will accompany students to guest speakers and facilitate discussions at lunch.

3.) Offer advice and mentorship!

Mentors are crucial in making the symposium a great experience for every student. Please answer questions and offer advice about majors, research, career paths, etc.!

Sign up by selecting 'Mentor' on our Eventbrite here or visiting stemjunction.eventbrite.com!

Shania Khatri

Founder, Senior Chair

Shania is a senior at Fox Chapel Area High School in Pittsburgh, PA. She's passionate about computer science and imagines a future where computer algorithms help us discover new and exciting things about the data we gather every day. Her research in bioinformatics at the University of Pittsburgh focuses on Bayesian modeling for novel gene discovery and patient-specific clinical modeling for pneumonia. In addition to conducting research, she's involved with various educational initiatives to promote bioinformatics and computer science.

Megan Cicconi

Faculty Sponsor

Megan was recently appointed Director of Instructional & Innovative Leadership at the Fox Chapel Area School District. Megan provided professional development to forty-two school districts in western Pennsylvania in the areas of literacy, curriculum development, & STEAM learning. She led the Center for Creativity, managed $2.6 million in STEAM grants, and created, facilitated, tracked learning in TransformED. She created the first K-12 STEAM Lending Library in the nation and organized the STEAM Showcase. Currently Megan steers Fox Chapel's innovative and instructional initiatives. She is especially involved in identifying equitable opportunities for students to thrive while partnering with other districts and organizations for the betterment of all youth. She recently rewrote the elementary computer science curriculum for grades K-6. Megan serves on the Carnegie Mellon University Create LAB Satellite Board, the Sprout Fund Board, the Arts Educational Collaborative Advisor Board, and is the co-founder and board member of the Change Machine.
