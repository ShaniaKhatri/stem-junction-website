What we do

STEM Junction is an annual bioinformatics symposium for advanced high school students in the Pittsburgh area. Bioinformatics is a new field that uses computer science and math to revolutionize the way biologists do research. STEM Junction is packed with talks on the newest discoveries and applications in sub-fields such as machine learning, computational modeling, gene discovery, electronic health records, data science ethics, and many more! STEM Junction Symposium is designed for high school students and is the field trip that influence career decisions and will change lives. No prior experience in computer science or biology is necessary!

Shania Khatri

Founder, Senior Chair

Shania is a senior at Fox Chapel Area High School in Pittsburgh, PA. She's passionate about computer science and imagines a future where computer algorithms help us discover new and exciting things about the data we gather every day. Her research in bioinformatics the University of Pittsburgh focuses on Bayesian modeling for novel gene discovery and patient-specific clinical modeling for pneumonia. In addition to conducting research, she's involved with various educational initiatives to promote bioinformatics and computer science.

Megan Cicconi

Faculty Sponsor

Megan was recently appointed Director of Instructional & Innovative Leadership at the Fox Chapel Area School District. She has held many regional leadership positions including her most recent position: Director of Instructional Innovation at the AIU. Megan provided professional development to forty-two school districts in western Pennsylvania in the areas of literacy, curriculum development, & STEAM learning. She led the Center for Creativity (Grable & Benedum Foundations funded), managed $2.6 million in STEAM grants, and created, facilitated, tracked learning in TransformED. Megan created the first K-12 STEAM Lending Library in the nation (Pittsburgh Penguins Foundation funded), where class-sets of technology resources are borrowed by educators after innovative professional development workshops to infuse STEAM into the classroom. Megan organized the STEAM Showcase (2014-2016) where schools & organizations showcased innovative learning to nearly 800 people. Currently Megan steers Fox Chapel's innovative and instructional initiatives. She is especially involved in identifying equitable opportunities for students to thrive while partnering with other districts and organizations for the betterment of all youth. With a fantastic team of FCASD educators, Megan has rewritten the elementary computer science infusing digital citizenship, computational thinking, and robotics into grades K-6. She received a $60,000 grant from the Grable Foundation to infuse digital manufacturing via a Mobile Fab Lab into regional elementary schools, and is currently writing a new STEM curriculum for elementary. This fall, the Fox Chapel Area School District was inducted into the prestigious League of Innovative Schools for the national leadership in the field of educational innovation. Megan serves on the Carnegie Mellon University Create LAB Satellite Board, the Sprout Fund Board, the Arts Educational Collaborative Advisor Board, and is the co-founder and board member of the Change Machine. 2015-2016 Honors: White House Maker Forum appointment, Open Portfolio Project appointment, IUP Doctoral Candidate; Digital Promise (Innovator Honorable Mention), Code.org (National Facilitator Communicator Award), and White House (Nominee Champion of Change for Making).
